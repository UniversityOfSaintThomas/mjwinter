Course Syllabus

Introduction

In this course we will grapple with some of the fundamental questions related to human conduct.    How we should conduct ourselves?  There are many different ways that we could respond to this question-- we will look at some of the most important approaches.  We will see that some of them are better than others. 

One of the aims of this course is to consider important aspects of some different moral theories to put us in a position to determine what a plausible moral theory might look like.  Another of our aims is to grapple with concrete moral questions that arise in disputes about lying, warfare, euthanasia and abortion etc..

You can find out more about the course procedure here.

More detailed information about the course is found on the course topics page.  This page contains links to assignments and audio lectures for both units of the course.  This material will be modified and updated as the course progresses, so ask before you work ahead.  In Unit 1 we will examine the foundation of ethical theory.  Is it plausible to think that there are objective truths in ethics that are binding for all human beings?  Once we have dealt with that questions, we will look at the theories of John Stuart Mill, Immanuel Kant, and Thomas Aquinas to get some ideas about how to construct a moral theory. 

In Unit 2 we learn some of the most central concepts involved in Aristotle's virtue theory.  We will spend more time on this theory than on any of the other theories because I find Aristotle's theory to be remarkably plausible and applicable. 

In Unit 3 we focus directly on issues such as just war, pornography, euthanasia, and abortion.

Grading
 

·          You will asked to write 4 short analytic papers on topics related to course content.  Each paper is to be written according to a format that is explained on the page about papers.  Each paper is worth 20 points, which constitutes 40% of your final grade

·          Regular participation in our discussion forum in Blackboard will be critical component of this course.  You will be required to make a minimum of 5 contributions per week to the forum.  A maximum of 5 points are awarded for participation for each week of class.  Participation is graded in terms of quantity and quality.  I want to see posts spread out over the week.  I would like some of your posts to be responses to other students, and for some to be questions or comments of your own.  5 postings brief postings on Sunday night will earn a low participation score for that week.  5 postings spread throughout the week that show you have reflected on the material will earn a high score for that week.  Participation constitutes 27.5% of the final grade

·          Weekly assignments are comprised of questions about the readings we will cover.  Assignments are worth 25% of the final grade.  Each assignment is worth a maximum of 5 points.  I will provide these assignments at the start of each week.  5 points will be awarded for exemplary work.  4 for good work.  3 for fair work.  2 for poor. 1 for late work.   Thorough, reflective responses to weekly assignment questions are expected, but this may not be sufficient to earn a score of 5, which is reserved for truly outstanding work. 

·          There will be 3 logic quizzes near the start of the course to make sure that students have basic skill needed for writing the papers.  These times quizzes will be worth 5 points each.  These quizzes are worth 7.5% of your final grade.
