 Phil 214: Introductory Ethics

In this course we will grapple with some of the fundamental questions related to human conduct.  In general, since all human beings share certain important characteristics, we want to be in a position to intelligently think about how we should conduct ourselves.  There are many different ways that we could respond to this question-- we will look at some of the most important approaches.  We will see that some of them are better than others. 

One of the aims of this course is to consider which moral theory is most adequate.  Another aim is to consider some moral concrete questions such as: Are we ever morally justified in taking the life of an innocent human being?  What if the consequences of taking a life are beneficial?  How about if the person is suffering gravely?  Must we always tell the truth?
