     Phil 214: Introductory Ethics


    First Exam Study Guide

    The midterm exam will cover all of our course material for Unit 1.    It will consist of several objective questions: true/false, multiple choice, fill in the blank, and short answer questions.

    Be familiar with the the general framework for the first unit of the course.  What is the project of the course?  How do different types of relativism pose a challenge to the project?  How can these challenges be met?  Be familiar with LaFollette's argument concerning moral relativism.   How does LaFollette compare language and morality?  What are the shortcomings of Lafollette's argument?  Be familiar with my argument against subjectivism.

    Be familiar with the material on Mill's utilitarianism. (I might provide a short passage and ask you to identify the author of it.)  Be familiar with the Mill handout.  What are some serious objections to utilitarianism?  What are some of the valuable aspects of utilitarianism?   Why does it utilitarianism fail as a moral theory.

    Be familiar with the material on Kant's theory.  Be familiar with the handout I provide.  What are some problems with the first formulation of the CI?  Why is the second formulation not subject to these criticisms?  What is Kant's view about lying?  Why? 

    Be familiar with the material from Aquinas.   How is the natural law structured? In what way can the natural law change?  Is the natural law always true?  Does everyone know it? What is Aquinas' view about killing?  murder?  lying?  Why?
