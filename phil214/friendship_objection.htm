Friendship Objection

Phil214

 

 

1) If pleasure and utility friendships are not genuine forms of friendship, then friendship based on virtue is the only genuine form of friendship.

 

2) But if friendship based on virtue is the only genuine form of friendship, then very few relationships will be genuine friendships.

 

3) Pleasure and utility friendships are not genuine forms of friendship.

 

4) So, very few relationships will be genuine friendships.

 

5) But genuine friendship is necessary for a good and happy life.

 

6) Therefore, almost nobody will have a good and happy life.

 

7) But if almost nobody will have a good and happy life, then a moral theory that implies this is practically useless.

 

8) Aristotle's moral theory is practically useless.
