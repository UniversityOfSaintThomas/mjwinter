     Phil 214: Introductory Ethics

    Paper #2
    Abortion

    In the last paper you are to present the strongest argument you can find from one side of the issue of abortion.   You should choose your opponent's best argument for the one you will present in the beginning of the paper.  Make use of any material from this course that supports the premises of the argument you present.  The argument you present for your opponent's view should be forceful and somewhat persuasive.  You will have to be dispassionate to carry out this task successfully.  For the purposes of this assignment, I am not interested in which view you ultimately accept, but in how well you can present and challenge your opponent's view while defending your own.  Your paper will be judged, in part, by how well you incorporate some of the strongest ideas we have discussed and read.

    More specifically, in Section I of your paper, present a formally valid two-premise argument for the conclusion of your opponent's argument. Explain and justify each premise of the argument you present.  Use as much material from this course as is relevant to the premises of the argument.  I will be looking for reference to material we have been using to discuss this issue.

    In Section II of the paper you are to critique the argument presented in from Section I.  Which premise of the argument is false?  Why?  Present an example of your own that illustrates why the premise is false.
