      Please respond to these questions.

    1.  What does Kant mean when he says "Nothing can possibly be conceived in the world, or even out of it, which can be called good, without qualification, except a good will?"  Explain.  Do you think he  is right about this?  Why or why not?

    2. Explain is the difference between hypothetical and categorical imperatives.

    3. Which of Kant's 4 examples best establishes his point?  Explain why.

    4. Do you find Kant's claim that every rational nature is an end in itself plausible?  Why/why not?  Mention some moral principles that might follow from this.

    5.  Explain at least two ways in which you think Kant's approach to moral theory differs from Mill's.
