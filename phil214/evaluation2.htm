IDEA Form Goals for Phil 214

 

 

Goal 2: Learning fundamental principles, generalizations or theories.

  • Focus on the theories in the first portion of the course (Relativism, Mill, Kant, Aquinas, Aristotle)
  • Focus on fundamental aspects within 4 main theories:
    1. Mill—Greatest Happiness Principle          
    2. Kant—Categorical Imperative
    3. Aquinas—Natural Inclinations/First Precept
    4. Aristotle—Definition of Happiness/Virtue/Function

 

Goal 11: Learning to analyze and critically evaluate ideas, arguments and points of view.

  • Constant focus on arguments in daily classroom discussions.  Is the argument sound?  Which premise is false?  Why?
  • Focus critical evaluation of arguments in papers.

 

 

Goal 10: Developing a clearer understanding of, and commitment to, personal values.

  • Lots of examples and discussions within the study of each moral theory to help us better connect abstract theories to commitments and decisions in our own lives.
  • Focus on issues in applied ethics in the last part of the course is designed to show that philosophical ethics can effect how we think about difficult and complex issues we may face.
