Paper #1
 

Please carefully read sections I-IV of:

  •   A Right to Choose Death? A moral argument for the permissibility of euthanasia and physician-assisted suicide by Frances Kamm.  

 

Your assignment is to present and defend Kamm's best argument for the permissibility of euthanasia, and then to critique the argument you have presented making use of articles discussed in class and your own ideas.  More specifically, in Section I of your paper, present a formally valid two-premise argument for the conclusion "Euthanasia can be morally permissible."  Explain and justify each premise of the argument you present using Kamm's article and any other information you take to support his premises.

 

In Section II of the paper you are to critique the argument presented in from Section I.  Which premise of the argument is false?  Why?  How do ideas discussed in class relate to the premises of the argument?  Present an example of your own that illustrates why a premise is false.  This paper should not exceed 425 words.  It is due by the end of the day Friday, April 9.
