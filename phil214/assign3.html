Readings

Utilitarianism by John Stuart Mill in Exploring Ethics p.92-103.

 

 

Here is a general summary handout:
The Basics of Utilitarianism


1. Mill claims that pleasure and freedom from pain are the only things desirable as ends.  Why would he claim this?.  Provide an example that supports his idea.
2. Explain what Mill means by "competent judges."  What role do these judges play in his theory?
3. Mill claims that "the motive has nothing to do with the morality of the action."  What do you think he means by this and why might he say it?
4. Mill speaks of proofs in ethics.  Does he believe ethical principles can be proven?  Explain.  Do you think ethical principles can be proven?  Why/why not?
