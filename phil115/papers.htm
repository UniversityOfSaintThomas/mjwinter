Philosophy of the Human Person

Papers

There are two short papers required for this course. Papers should be about 3 (double spaced) pages in length. Written assignments are intended to provide an opportunity for you to present and assess an argument.  Organization will be weighed heavily, and grammar will count. Each paper will be worth 15% of your final grade. 
 

Assignments

Paper #1

Below is an argument attempting to prove that we are not brains in vats.  There is a problem with this argument.  Where is the problem? I want you to think about this argument in connection with Descartes’ Meditations.

I.                     Briefly explain in one paragraph or less how this argument connects with Descartes’ Meditations and  how the argument below attempts to respond to Descartes’ concerns. 

II.                   Provide a brief explanation of each premise of this argument in your own words. 

III.       Explain which premise is false and why it is false.  Use an example of your own to illustrate why the false premise is false. 

If you think that one of the conditional statements in the argument is false, then you will need to remember that a conditional statement is false only when its antecedent is true but its consequent is false.  So, “If it rains, then the ground is wet” is false only when it rains but the ground is not wet. 

Your paper should not exceed three double spaced pages in length.  Remember to remain focused on the argument. 

An Argument Against the Possibility of Being a Brain in a Vat:

Presupposition: A statement is true if and only if the terms in a statement refer to the realities they designate.  A statement is false or meaningless otherwise. 

For example, ‘Grass is green’ is true if and only if grass is green. 

1) Either we are brains in a vat or we are not brains in a vat.
Either -B or B   (assumption)

2) If  we are not brains in a vat, then we know that the sentence ‘We are brains in a vat’ is false.
If-B, then F  (assumption)

3) If we are brains in a vat, then we know that the sentence ‘We are brains in a vat’ is false.
If B, then F  (assumption)

4) So we know that the sentence ‘We are brains in a vat’ is false.
F         (from 1,2,3)

5) If we know that the sentence ‘We are brains in a vat’ is false, then we know that we are not brains in vats.
If F, then K   (from presupposition)

6) So we know that we are not brains in vats.
K    (from 4,5)
 
 

Paper #2

Please read "Why People Think Computers Can't" by Marvin Minsky.  Your assignment is to construct, explain and evaluate Minsky's main argument. This paper should not exceed 3 pages.

1) Find Minsky's main conclusion.  What is the point of his article? 

2) Construct Minsky's main argument.  Find two premises that support the conclusion in (1) to make a valid argument.  I invite you to check with me after you construct this argument to make sure that you are on the right track. 

3) Explain the premises of Minsky's argument.  Use the article to support the premises you use for the main argument.  Explain what each of the premises means in your own words. 

4) Evaluate Minsky's argument.  Now that you have explained the premises of Minsky's argument, explain why the argument is sound or not.  Is the first premise true/false?  Why or why not?  Is the second premise true or false?  Why or why not?  Explain in your own words why you think Minsky's argument succeeds or fails.  Use examples of your own to support what you say.
