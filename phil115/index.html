Course Description

Philosophy 115 is designed to help students acquire the logic skills needed to evaluate arguments and texts concerned with different aspects of human nature. This course will serve its purpose if it fosters an appreciation for philosophical thought, respect for the thinkers we will study, and the desire to apply the skills and ideas learned to the area of ethics. 

The questions we will address include: Is there such a thing as truth? Do human beings have a specific nature? Are human beings free? Do human beings have a soul? If so, could the human soul be immortal? Are human beings merely a sum of physical parts and processes? 

Our course topics are drawn from the works of some great figures in the history of thought. Most of the readings are not lengthy, but they are challenging. I expect that every student read each piece of material at least twice. 

Course Requirements

(1) Exams:  The mid-term and final will be comprised of essay and short answer questions. More about these exams as the course unfolds. 

(2) Papers: There are two short papers required for this course. Papers should be about 3 (double spaced) pages in length. Papers are intended to provide an opportunity for you to present and assess an argument. Organization will be weighed heavily, and grammar will count. Each paper will be worth 15% of  your final grade. 

(3) Quizzes: There will be 10 five-question quizzes pertaining to logic. These quizzes are worth 10% of the final grade. These quizzes will be dispersed throughout the semester.

(4) Class participation: Participation in class discussion is  worth 15% of your grade. 

(5) Assignments: I regularly assign study questions that are due prior to our next class meeting.  These assignments are graded on a  check +, check, check-   scale. Assignments are worth 15% of your final grade.
