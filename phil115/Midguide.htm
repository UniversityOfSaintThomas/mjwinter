Phil 115
Midterm Study Guide

This study guide is not intended to be an exhaustive list of items for the exam, but as a guide.

  • I want you to be familiar with the structure of the Phaedo.  You should have a sense for the order in which the arguments in the dialogue appear.
  • You should be prepared to present, explain and evaluate Socrates' argument for the conclusion that philosophers accept death willingly from the Phaedo.
  • You should be prepared to present, explain and evaluate the recollection argument from the Phaedo.
  • You should be prepared to present, explain and evaluate the final argument for the immortality of the soul from the Phaedo.
  • Be comfortable responding to all of the study questions assigned for Descartes.
  • Be prepared to explain Descartes’ general project in his Meditations.  What types of knowledge does he consider?  What doubt is raised with respect to each type?  Why does he propose to doubt everything?
  • What is the first thing Descartes knows with certainty?  Why?  Explain the relevance of his wax example.
  • You should be prepared to present, explain and evaluate the argument attempting to prove God’s existence. Be prepared to offer a forceful criticism of the argument.
  • How does Descartes respond to each of his doubts from Meditation 1?  Are these responses satisfactory?
