PHILOSOPHICAL LEXICON

*Four causes-

Intrinsic

1. Material- stuff (potentiality)

2. Formal- structure (actuality)

Extrinsic

3. Efficient- mover

4. Final- goal (teleology)

Potency/Actuality- In Aristotle and St. Thomas, the soul is defined in terms of these two concepts. The distinction between matter/form is analogous.

Potency- unfinished, indeterminate, imperfect, incomplete

Actuality- finished, determinate, perfect, complete a) first act- possession of a power (e.g. thought) b) second act- exercise of that power (e.g. thinking about philosophy)

Substance/Accident- Human beings are examples of composite substances (see Aristotelian ontology).

Substance- that which exists in itself

Accident- that which exists in another (i.e. a substance)

Essential/Accidental Properties-

Essential- those attributes which a thing must have to be what it is (e.g. rationality for humans)

Accidental- those attributes which a thing might lack but remain the same thing (e.g. six feet tall)

Definition of Soul- first actuality of a body having life potentially. Soul is the formal cause of the human composite which is a substance; body is the material cause. (God is the efficient cause; God's providential plan is the final cause)
