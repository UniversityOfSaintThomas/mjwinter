Philosophy of the Human Person

Course Procedure

Although this course is being offered completely over the Internet, I am setting the same standards that apply to any of the courses I offer at UST.  Because it is easier to fall behind without regular class meetings, it is especially important that you pay careful attention to the deadlines that are set for all course work.  I will try to provide ample time for completion of all assignments.  I will use our Discussion Forum as the main tool for conveying information about assignments and due dates.  You can also get in touch with me via email mjwinter@stthomas.edu
if some emergency or problem arises that you think I should know about.

The routine that we will follow will usually consist in this process:

  • I assign a text to be read.
  • I provide an assignment to accompany the reading material.
  • I ask you to turn in assignments (via email) by a specific time.
Once you have worked on responses to all of the questions:
  • Listen to audio files that relate to the reading.
  • Make contributions to the discussion forum on the topic being addressed.
I expect that the Discussion Forum will be the place where most of the teaching and learning will take place in this course.  This is the best place for us to interact with one another.  Any thoughts, ideas, and questions about course topics should be posted there.  Email should usually be used for questions and comments that do not relate directly to course topics.

You class participation grade will be based on your contibutions to the discussion forum.  I will take the quantity and quality of your postings into consideration when determining this portion of your grade.  I expect you to make no less than 5 postings in the forum per week.  This is a minimum expectation.  Your postings should be spread throughout the week.

I am really looking forward to working through this material with you.  I expect that this course will be a great learning experience for all of us.
