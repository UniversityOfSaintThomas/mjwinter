Study Topics for the Final

Phil380

 

The list of topics/questions below is not intended to be completely exhaustive, but it should serve as a good study guide.

 

Bonjour’s Rationalism: How does Bonjour’s moderate rationalism provide a model for foundationalism?  How are the most basic claims known according to this account?  Why must there be a priori knowledge?  Why does moderate empiricism fail?  Why does Bonjour argue that the basic claims are fallible?  What are some implications of this approach?  What are some advantages and disadvantages of moderate rationalism compared to pure rationalism?

 

Bonjour’s Foundationalism:  What are the problems with foundationalism?  How does Bonjour address them?  What is a serious problem with coherentism?  How does the foundationalist have an advantage over the coherentist?  How might Aristotle’s account of how we know foundational principles be seen to fit with Bonjour’s account of this matter?

 

Bonjour on Induction: What is the problem of induction and why is the lack of a good solution a scandal?  How does Bonjour propose to solve the problem?  In what way is Bonjour’s solution a priori?  What are the likely criticisms of the solution?

 

Bonjour on Internalism: What is internalism and what are some problems with it?  What is externalism, and what are some problems with it?  In what way might internalists and externalists live happily side by side?  Why is internalism the superior view according to BonJour?

 

Goldman: What is Goldman’s more recent account of knowledge (the one designed to deal with barn façade-type cases)?  How does the analysis apply to such a case?  What are some strengths and shortcomings of this approach? 

 

Moore: What is Moore’s proof of the external world?  What conditions does Moore associate with proof?  Does Moore’s proof count as an Aristotelian proof?   Why/why not? 

 

Aristotle: What conditions does Aristotle associate with proof?  What criteria do premises of demonstrations have to satisfy?  How are the ultimate premises of demonstration justified?  How can Aristotle’s theory be understood as a type of weak or moderate foundationalism?

 

Kim: What is Quine’s argument against traditional epistemology?  Explain its premises?  What does it mean to say that traditional epistemology is normative, and why is normativity problematic in Quine’s view? What is naturalized epistemology?  What are its problems?

 

Nozick: How does Nozick’s account of knowledge enable him to respond to skepticism?  How, specifically does he address the skeptic?  What is the closure principle and how does the skeptic use it?  How does Nozick respond to the skeptic?

 

Vogel: How is the zebra case supposed to show that closure fails?  How about the car theft case?  How are the cases different?  What is Vogel’s criticism of each case?

 

Final response: What is the best way to address the skeptic using moderate rationalism?  Is this a refutation of skepticism?  How does the skeptic carry the burden of proof?
