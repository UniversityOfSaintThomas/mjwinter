The Argument for Phenomenalism

 

1)   If (a)Perceptual Subjectivism and the (b)Humean Thesis are true, and (c)there is knowledge of ordinary objects, then phenomenalism is the best explanation of our perceptual knowledge.

2)   (a) Perceptual Subjectivism is true

3)   (b) The Humean Thesis is correct

4)   (c) There is knowledge of ordinary objects

5)   So, phenomenalism is the best explanation of our perceptual knowledge.

 

Which premise is most vulnerable?

 

 

 

The Argument for Representative Realism

 

1)   The realm of immediate sensory experience, of sense data, is (d) too orderly not to demand an explanation, and (e) not orderly enough for that explanation to be that the sensory data have an intrinsic order of their own.

2)   If (d), then representative realism provides the best explanation of our immediate sensory experience.

3)   If (e), then phenomenalism does not provide the best explanation of our immediate sensory experience.

4)   So, representative realism provides the best explanation of our immediate sensory experience and phenomenalism does not provide the best explanation of our immediate sensory experience.
