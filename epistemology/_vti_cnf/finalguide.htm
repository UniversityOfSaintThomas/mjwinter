Study Topics for the Final

Phil380

 

The list of topics/questions below is not intended to be completely exhaustive, but it should serve as a good study guide.

 

 

Goldman: What is Goldman’s more recent account of knowledge (the one designed to deal with barn façade-type cases)?  How does the analysis apply to such a case?  What are some strengths and shortcomings of this approach? 

 

Aristotle: What conditions does Aristotle associate with proof?  What criteria do premises of demonstrations have to satisfy?  How are the ultimate premises of demonstration justified?  How can Aristotle’s theory be understood as a type of weak or moderate foundationalism?

 

Moore: What is Moore’s proof of the external world?  What conditions does Moore associate with proof?  Does Moore’s proof count as an Aristotelian proof?   Why/why not? 

 

Bonjour on Induction: What is the problem of induction and why is the lack of a good solution a scandal?  What is the pragmatic vindication?  How does Bonjour propose to solve the problem?  In what way is Bonjour’s solution a priori?  What are the likely criticisms of the solution?

 

Bonjour on Moderate Rationalism: How does Bonjour’s moderate rationalism provide a model for foundationalism?  What are the serious criticisms of rationalism?  How can these criticisms be addressed? How are basic claims known according to this account? What are some implications of this approach?  

 

Bonjour’s on Foundationalism:  What is the regress problem?  How does foundationalism attempt to solve this problem?  What are the problems with coherentism?  What are the problems with foundationalism?  How can foundationalism deal with these problems?

 

Bonjour on Internalism:  What distinguishes internalism from externalism?  What are the problems with externalism/reliabilism?  What are the problems with internalism?  In what way might internalisms and externalists be speaking past one another?  Why is internalism unavoidable?

 

Naturalized Epistemology: What is Quine’s argument against traditional epistemology?  Explain its premises?  What does it mean to say that traditional epistemology is normative, and why is normativity problematic in Quine’s view? What is naturalized epistemology?  What are its problems?

 

Skepticism:  How does Nozick’s portray the skeptic’s argument?  How, specifically does he address the skeptic’s argument?  What are the counter-examples to the closure principle?  Do these succeed?  What is the best argument against the skeptic?  What does this argument show?

 

Audi on Science, Morality and Religion: How does reasoning proceed in science? What role do induction, deduction, imagination and inference to the best explanation play in science?  What is moral non-cognitivism? What are the problems with it? What is ethical intuitionism?  Is religious knowledge possible?  How?  Is religion on epistemically weaker ground than science or religion?
